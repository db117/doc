# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

The primary user is the site owner and technically oriented visitors looking for practical notes, reference links, AI
tooling records, Java, database, operations, and OS documentation. They usually arrive with a concrete task and need to
scan quickly, search, or jump into a known category.

## Product Purpose

This VitePress site publishes a personal technical knowledge base and curated navigation hub. Success means useful
material is easy to find, new notes can be added without ceremony, and the home page works as a compact launchpad rather
than a promotional landing page.

## Brand Personality

Practical, direct, engineering-first. The tone should feel like a maintained personal workbench: concise, useful, and
calm, with enough structure to make repeated visits efficient.

## Anti-references

Avoid marketing-style landing pages, oversized decorative heroes, heavy card galleries, vague slogans, and visual
effects that slow down scanning. Avoid designs that make the navigation feel like a product brochure instead of a
working technical index.

## Design Principles

- Make retrieval faster than discovery: search, categories, and link grouping should help a returning user jump straight
  to the right place.
- Preserve the lightweight authoring model: Markdown should remain easy to maintain and avoid brittle page-specific
  markup where possible.
- Prefer dense but calm structure: show many useful links without making the page feel noisy.
- Let personal context stay visible: the site should feel maintained by a real technical practitioner, not a generic
  documentation template.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Aim for WCAG AA contrast and keyboard-friendly navigation. Motion should be minimal and respect reduced-motion
preferences. Chinese text, mixed English tool names, and long technical labels should wrap cleanly on mobile.
